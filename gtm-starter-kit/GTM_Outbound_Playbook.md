## How to Use This Playbook

This is a complete B2B cold outbound system. It's not theory — it's the exact framework used to build real pipelines from zero. Replace the bracketed [placeholders] with your company's details and start sending.

---

## 1. Define Your ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

Before writing a single email, answer these:

| Question | Your Answer |
|----------|-------------|
| What do you sell? | [Your product/service] |
| Who feels the pain most? | [Job title + company type] |
| What happens if they don't fix this? | [Cost of inaction] |
| What's your price point? | [£/$ range] |
| What's your sales cycle? | [Days/weeks to close] |

### ICP Segmentation — 3 Tiers

Not all prospects are equal. Prioritise ruthlessly:

| Tier | Who | Why They're Priority |
|------|-----|---------------------|
| 🔴 **Tier 1** | Companies where your product solves an urgent, measurable problem | They have budget pressure, active pain, or a trigger event |
| 🟡 **Tier 2** | Companies that benefit from your product but aren't under pressure today | They care about the problem but it's not on fire |
| 🟢 **Tier 3** | Companies that could use your product but have long sales cycles or low urgency | Worth planting seeds — don't spend your best hours here |

**Rule:** Start every Monday with Tier 1 only. Move to Tier 2 when Tier 1 is worked.

## 2. Pain Signals — What to Look For

When researching a prospect, these signals tell you what angle to lead with:

| Signal | What It Means | Email Angle |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| They're hiring for a role you make easier | Active pain — they're investing to solve this | "I saw you're hiring for [role] — we help [solve X]" |
| Recent funding round | Budget available, pressure to grow | "Congrats on the raise — [pain] usually hits around this stage" |
| Competitor just launched a feature | Competitive pressure | "With [competitor] doing X, you might be thinking about [Y]" |
| Negative reviews mentioning your area | Customer-facing pain | Reference it carefully in the email |
| New leadership in your buyer's role | New leader = new budget, new priorities | "New in role? Most [titles] we talk to are rethinking [X]" |
| Seasonal peak approaching | Time pressure | "With [season/event] coming up, [problem] gets worse" |

## 3. Personalisation Stack

Before writing any email, find one specific thing about the company. Score your personalisation:

| Quality | What It Looks Like | Example |
|---------|-------------------|---------|
| 🟢 **Strong** | Something specific from their site, LinkedIn, or news | "Saw your Q1 launch of [feature] — [relevant connection]" |
| 🟡 **OK** | Company model or positioning that shows you read their site | "Your [pricing model] means [implication]" |
| 🔴 **Weak** | Generic reference that could apply to anyone | "I see you're in [industry]" — DELETE THIS |

**Rule:** If you can remove the personalised opener and the email still works perfectly, the personalisation was decoration. Rewrite it.

## 4. Subject Line Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **4-6 words maximum** | Anything longer gets cropped on mobile |
| **All lowercase** | Looks like an internal email, not a marketing blast |
| **Include company name OR a specific reference** | "[company] + [topic]" beats "[generic benefit]" |
| **Never put YOUR company name in the subject** | Your brand in a cold subject = spam signal |
| **No question marks** | "Improving your outcomes?" = marketing language |
| **Touch 4 subject** | Standalone insight — no company name needed |
| **Touch 5 subject** | Always "closing the loop" — short, neutral, curiosity-driven |

### Examples

| Touch 1 | Touch 4 | Touch 5 |
|---------|---------|---------|
| [company] and [topic] | [industry insight headline] | closing the loop |
| [topic] for [company] grads | the [problem] most [audience] ignore | closing the loop |
| [company]'s [model] and [your topic] | what [buyers] actually [do/test/want] | closing the loop |

## 5. The 5-Touch Sequence

Every outreach sequence is 5 emails over 22 days. **Do not compress this timeline.**

| Touch | Day | Thread | Goal | Max Words |
|-------|-----|--------|------|-----------|
| **T1** | Day 0 | New | Cold open — problem + solution + CTA | 120 |
| **T2** | Day 3 | Reply to T1 | Value proof — one specific insight, no throat-clearing | 80 |
| **T3** | Day 8 | Reply to T1 | New angle — flip the frame, soft ask | 80 |
| **T4** | Day 14 | NEW thread | Insight email — thought leadership, no hard sell | 100 |
| **T5** | Day 22 | NEW thread | Breakup — 1/2/3 option format | 60 |

### T1 — The Cold Open (Template)

> Hi [Name],
>
> [One specific observation about their company — strong personalisation].
>
> I run [Your Company], [one sentence on what you do]. We [specific thing you do] — [why it matters to them].
>
> [One sentence connecting your product to their specific situation].
>
> Worth 20 minutes this week?
>
> [Your name]
> [Your company] | [your website]
> Book a call: [your calendly]

### T2 — The Value Proof (Template)

> Hi [Name],
>
> Quick follow-up — [one specific insight or data point relevant to their world].
>
> [One sentence connecting that insight back to your product].
>
> Happy to walk through how we approach this if useful.
>
> [Your name]

### T3 — The Angle Flip (Template)

> Hi [Name],
>
> Different angle — [flip the frame. If T1 was about their problem, T3 is about their customer/user/employee].
>
> [One sentence with a new reason to care].
>
> Open to a quick call?
>
> [Your name]

### T4 — The Insight Drop (Template)

> Hi [Name],
>
> [Share a genuine insight about their industry — something you've learned from working in this space. No pitch.]
>
> [Connect it to a broader pattern you're seeing].
>
> [If relevant, mention how your product fits — but make it feel like an aside, not the point].
>
> [Your name]

### T5 — The Breakup (Copy This Exactly)

> Hi [Name],
>
> Didn't want to keep chasing if the timing's off or it's not a fit.
>
> Three options — reply with a number:
>
> 1. Interested — let's find 20 minutes this week
> 2. Not now — happy to check back in a few months
> 3. Not a fit — good to know either way
>
> [Your name]

**Why this works:** You're not asking them to compose a rejection — you're asking them to press a key. Response rates on breakup emails using this format are 2-3× higher than standard bumps.

## 6. LinkedIn Sequence

Run this alongside email:

| Day | Action | Template |
|-----|--------|----------|
| Day 0 | Connection request | "Hi [Name] — I work in [space] and [Company] caught my attention. Would be great to connect." |
| Day 7 | DM (if connected) | "Thanks for connecting. I work with [type of company] on [problem] — thought there might be a fit. Open to a quick chat?" |

**Rules:**
- Never pitch in the connection request
- Keep DMs to 2 sentences max
- If they don't connect, don't message — move on

## 7. Voice & Tone Rules

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|------|---------|
| Write under 120 words for T1 | Write more than 150 words for any touch |
| Use the contact's first name once | Use it multiple times in one email |
| Start with a specific observation | Start with "I hope this email finds you well" |
| End with a single clear ask | End with "Let me know if you have any questions" |
| Write in short paragraphs (1-2 sentences) | Write dense blocks of text |
| State the business case plainly | Use phrases like "I'd love to", "I truly believe" |

### Words to Delete From Every Email

- "I'd love to chat"
- "Just following up"
- "I truly believe"
- "Honestly" / "Really" / "Actually"
- "I hope this finds you well"
- "Don't hesitate to reach out"
- "At your earliest convenience"
- "Please feel free to"
- "I wanted to reach out because"

## 8. Email Signature

```
[Your Name]
[Your Company] | [yourwebsite.com]
Book a call: [your calendly link]
```

3 lines. No logo. No marketing tagline. No "sent from my iPhone."

## 9. Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We do this in-house" | "Good to hear — is it [specific alternative]? Happy to chat about what we do differently." |
| "Our [audience] don't need this" | "That's the goal — curious what your current [metric] looks like?" |
| "No budget" | "Most of our partnerships start with a pilot. No long-term commitment needed." |
| "Not the right time" | "Totally fine — when would be better? I'll set a reminder." |
| "We use [competitor]" | "Good to know — what does that currently cover? Happy to share how we're different." |
| No response after 5 touches | Mark as "Dormant." Re-add to sequence in 90 days with a new angle. |

## 10. KPIs — What to Track Weekly

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| T1 emails sent per week | 10-20 new companies |
| Reply rate (any response) | > 10% |
| Positive reply rate | > 4% |
| Meetings booked | > 2% of T1s |
| Pipeline conversations per month | 5-10 warm |
| Closed deals per quarter | 2-4 |

**What counts as "positive":** Anything that isn't "not interested" or silence. "Not now," "send more info," "let's talk in a few months" — all positive. These go back into the sequence.

## 11. 30-Day Execution Plan

### Week 1: Launch
- Send T1 to all Tier 1 companies
- Send LinkedIn connection requests
- Log every send in your tracker

### Week 2: Follow Up + Expand
- Send T2 to all T1 recipients
- Identify 10 new Tier 2 companies
- Reply to any responses within 24 hours

### Week 3: Mid-Sequence + New Batch
- Send T3 to original batch
- Send T1 to new Tier 2 companies
- LinkedIn DMs to anyone who connected but didn't reply

### Week 4: Insight + Breakup
- Send T4 to original batch
- Send T2/T3 to Tier 2 batch
- Review what's working — which angles get replies?

### Day 30 Review:
- Count: emails sent, replies, meetings booked
- Identify: top-performing segments and angles
- Adjust: double down on what's working
