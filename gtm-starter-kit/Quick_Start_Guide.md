## Step 1: Define Your ICP (30 minutes)

Open `ICP_Worksheet.md` and fill in:

1. **What you sell** — one sentence, plain English
2. **Who feels the pain most** — job title + company type
3. **What happens if they don't fix this** — the cost of inaction
4. **3 tiers** — rank your target companies by urgency

Don't overthink this. You can refine later. The goal is to have a list of 15-20 companies to target in your first batch.

---

## Step 2: Find Your First 20 Targets (30 minutes)

For each target company, find:

| Field | Where to Find It |
|-------|-----------------|
| Company name | You already know this |
| Website | Google it |
| Contact name | LinkedIn search → filter by company + title |
| Contact title | LinkedIn profile |
| Contact email | Apollo.io (free) or Hunter.io (free) |
| LinkedIn URL | Copy from their profile |

**Can't find the contact's email?** Use this format and test: firstname@company.com, firstname.lastname@company.com. Most B2B emails follow one of these patterns.

---

## Step 3: Write Your First T1 Emails (30 minutes)

Open `Email_Sequence_Templates.md`. For each company:

1. Pick the template that matches their segment
2. Replace all [brackets] with their specific details
3. Add one line of strong personalisation (see the Playbook §3)
4. Check the subject line against the rules in `Subject_Line_Rulebook.md`
5. Keep it under 120 words

**Pro tip:** Write the first 3 emails manually. You'll find your voice. Then the rest go faster.

---

## Step 4: Set Up Your CRM (10 minutes)

Open `Pipeline_CRM.html` in your browser. It runs locally — no account needed.

1. Add your target companies
2. Set their stage to "Prospecting"
3. Add the contact name and email
4. Update the stage as you send emails and get responses

---

## Step 5: Send Day 1 Emails (20 minutes)

1. Open your email (Gmail, Outlook, or company email)
2. Copy the T1 email from your template
3. Paste into a new email
4. Set the subject line
5. Hit send
6. Log it in your tracker — mark T1 as "Sent" with today's date

**Do all Tier 1 companies first.** Then Tier 2 if you have time.

---

## Step 6: Follow the Sequence

After Day 1, your calendar looks like this:

| Day | What to Do |
|-----|-----------|
| Day 0 (today) | Send T1 + LinkedIn connection requests |
| Day 3 | Send T2 (reply to your T1 thread) |
| Day 7 | LinkedIn DM anyone who connected |
| Day 8 | Send T3 (reply to your T1 thread) |
| Day 14 | Send T4 (NEW email thread) |
| Day 22 | Send T5 breakup (NEW email thread) |

Set calendar reminders for each date. Don't skip days.

---

## Step 7: Handle Replies

When someone replies:

| They Said | You Do |
|-----------|--------|
| "Interested" / "Let's chat" | Send your Calendly link immediately |
| "Not now" / "Maybe later" | Thank them, ask when to follow up, set a reminder |
| "Send more info" | Send a 3-sentence summary + Calendly link |
| "Not interested" | Thank them, mark as Closed-Lost in CRM |
| No reply after T5 | Mark as Dormant, re-approach in 90 days |

**Response speed matters.** Reply within 2 hours during business hours. Same day at worst.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Writing long emails | Cut it in half. Then cut it again. |
| Skipping personalisation | Find ONE specific thing. It takes 2 minutes. |
| Sending all touches in one week | Follow the 22-day schedule. Urgency kills trust. |
| Only emailing, no LinkedIn | Run both channels. LinkedIn warms them up. |
| Not logging sends | If it's not tracked, it didn't happen. |
| Giving up after 1-2 touches | Most replies come on T3-T5. Keep going. |
